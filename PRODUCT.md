# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: 16-35 year old competitive tennis-ball cricket players who play on local grounds, dream of recognition, and consume esports and combat sports content. They want their sport taken seriously, their skills seen, and their journey from street to stadium validated.

Secondary: Tournament organizers needing a platform to run events, sponsors seeking engaged young audiences, team owners looking for talent discovery, and scouts identifying rising players.

## Product Purpose

Arena Clash is the future of tennis-ball cricket. It transforms local players into recognized athletes through a broadcast-quality ecosystem where competition, rivalry, and raw talent take center stage. Success means a player from any gully or ground can be discovered, followed, and celebrated like a professional.

## Brand Personality

Aggressive. Cinematic. Authentic. Voice is direct and charged with adrenaline, never corporate or sanitized. Speaks in short, punchy statements. Respects the player's intelligence and hunger. Emotional territory: pride, rivalry, recognition, the roar of floodlit nights.

## Anti-references

Do not look like: generic SaaS dashboards, fantasy cricket apps with cartoon graphics, betting websites, template sports websites. No corporate stock photography. No sanitized "sports entertainment" polish. No gradient text, glassmorphism, or side-stripe borders.

## Design Principles

1. Street to Stadium: Every visual element bridges the raw energy of local cricket with broadcast production quality. The dirt and grass of the ground meet the glare of floodlights and the precision of a scoreboard.
2. Rivalry as Fuel: Red versus Blue is not a color scheme, it is the dramatic engine. Every page should feel like a face-off. Tension, contrast, the moment before the bowl.
3. Esports Broadcast Aesthetic: Frame everything through the lens of a live broadcast. Lower thirds, player stat overlays, countdown clocks, and slow-motion hero shots. The page is a broadcast, not a brochure.
4. One Match at a Time: Focus on the immediate moment of competition. Stats matter. Scores matter. The next ball matters. Every page centers the action.
5. No Fake Hype: The energy comes from real stakes, real players, real competition. No stock imagery, no fake crowd noise, no empty promises. Authenticity over polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. High contrast for scoreboards and stats which must be legible at a glance. Caption all video content. Consider red-green color blindness in the Red vs Blue palette (use lightness and icon shape, not just hue).
